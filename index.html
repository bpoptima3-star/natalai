FAQ

Common Questions

What is a Vedic natal birth chart?
A Vedic natal chart is a precise map of the sky at the exact moment of your birth. It shows all 9 planets placed in 12 houses, each governing a different area of your life. Unlike Western astrology, Vedic charts use the sidereal zodiac — tracking real planetary positions against fixed stars, making it significantly more precise.
What's the difference between Vedic and Western astrology?
Western uses a tropical zodiac (based on seasons). Vedic uses a sidereal zodiac (based on actual star positions) — about 23 degrees different. Your Vedic sign is often different from your Western sign — typically shifted back by about 23 degrees. Vedic astrology also emphasises your Moon Sign over your Sun Sign, and uses a unique Planetary Period (Dasha) system to map your life timeline — something Western astrology doesn't have.
Do I need my exact birth time?
Birth time significantly improves accuracy — especially for your Rising Sign (Ascendant), which changes every 2 hours. Without exact time we still calculate your Moon Sign, planetary positions, and approximate Planetary Period. For paid reports, an accurate birth time is strongly recommended.
What is Vedic Soul Compatibility Reading?
Vedic Soul Compatibility Reading is the ancient astrological analysis of two birth charts for relationship harmony. It scores 8 key areas of compatibility — personality, values, health, nature, progeny potential, and longevity — out of 36 points. It also checks planetary harmony and conflict points between both charts.
How is NatalAI different from other astrology apps?
Most astrology apps use Western sun-sign astrology or simplified calculations. NatalAI uses authentic Vedic astrology — the same system used in India for over 5,000 years — powered by Swiss Ephemeris precision and AI. What are Planetary Combinations in Vedic astrology?
Planetary combinations are specific combinations of planets in your natal chart that indicate particular life outcomes and tendencies. Examples include Raja Yoga (power and success), Dhana Yoga (wealth), Gaja Kesari Yoga (wisdom and recognition), and Kala Sarpa formation (intense karmic focus). Your chart may contain multiple combinations, each shaping different areas of your destiny. Our AI detects and clearly explains each one.
